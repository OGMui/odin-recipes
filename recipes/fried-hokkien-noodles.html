Fried Hokkien Noodles

Golden Goodness Fried Hokkien Noodles Done Right!

Indulge in the tantalizing flavors of Southeast Asia with this exquisite recipe for Fried Hokkien Noodles. Begin by soaking thick Hokkien noodles until they're tender yet resilient to the bite. In a wok, infuse fragrant garlic and ginger with a medley of fresh vegetables such as crisp bell peppers, crunchy bean sprouts, and vibrant green onions, creating a symphony of colors and textures. Next, add succulent pieces of chicken or shrimp, sizzling until they're golden and perfectly cooked. Then, the star of the dish, the soaked noodles, join the wok, absorbing the savory essence of oyster sauce, soy sauce, and a hint of sweetness from dark caramel sauce. Toss everything together with a gentle yet precise hand, ensuring every strand of noodle is coated in the rich, umami-laden sauce. Finally, serve piping hot, garnished with a flourish of fragrant cilantro and a squeeze of zesty lime, inviting all to savor the harmonious blend of flavors in this culinary masterpiece.

Ingredients

  • 250g Hokkien noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 200g chicken breast or shrimp, sliced into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon dark caramel sauce (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Steps

  1. Prepare the Noodles:

    Start by soaking the Hokkien noodles in hot water according to the package instructions until they are tender but still slightly firm. Drain and set aside.

  2. Heat the Wok:

    Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering.

  3. Saut√© Aromatics:

    Add the minced garlic and ginger to the hot oil and stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant.

  4. Cook Protein:

    Add the sliced chicken breast or shrimp to the wok and stir-fry until they are cooked through and golden brown. Remove the cooked protein from the wok and set aside.

  5. Add Vegetables:

    In the same wok, add the sliced bell peppers and bean sprouts. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are tender yet still crisp.

  6. Combine Ingredients:

    Return the cooked protein to the wok along with the chopped green onions. Stir in the soaked Hokkien noodles.

  7. Add Sauce:

    Drizzle the oyster sauce, soy sauce, and dark caramel sauce (if using) over the noodles and toss everything together until well combined. Adjust the seasoning to taste.

  8. Finish and Serve:

    Continue stir-frying for another 2-3 minutes until the noodles are heated through and evenly coated with the sauce. Transfer the Fried Hokkien Noodles to serving plates, garnish with fresh cilantro, and serve hot with lime wedges on the side.
